**Symbolic AI** (also called **Good Old-Fashioned AI**, or GOFAI) is an approach to [[artificial intelligence]] that represents knowledge using symbols and rules. It treats intelligence as a process of manipulating structured data, like logic statements or if-then rules.

Symbolic AI has the following properties:
- **Knowledge is explicitly encoded**
- **Reasoning is rule-based**: Uses logic (e.g., propositional or predicate logic) to draw conclusions.
- **Programs are interpretable**: You can inspect and understand how decisions are made.

Symbolic AI was dominant from the 1950s to the 1980s, powering early expert systems. Its main limitation is brittleness—it struggles with ambiguity, uncertainty, and learning from raw data, unlike modern machine learning approaches.

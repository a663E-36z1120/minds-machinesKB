If our answer is positive to the question of [[Can Machines Think?]], we need to further consider the problem of **what is machine [[intelligence]]** (or [[Artificial Intelligence]]).

Modern consensus is that rather than the use of (strict, pre-programmed, symbolic, language-like) rules (i.e. [[Symbolic AI]]), machine intelligence emerge from experience-driven and "sub-symbolic" (i.e. [[Machine Learning]]) procedures that are dynamic and adaptive (see also: [[What Does It Mean to Learn?]]).

Aligns with symbolic, rule-based approach to machine intelligence:
- [[Michael Bratman]]: 'Intention, Plans, and Practical Reason'

Critics of rule-based approach to machine intelligence:
- [[Hubert Dreyfus]]
- [[Daniel Dennett]]: [[R1]] the symbolic reasoner.

If our answer is positive to the question of [[Can Machines Think?]], we need to further consider the problem of **what is machine intelligence**.

Modern consensus is that rather than the use of (strict, pre-programmed, symbolic, language-like) rules, machine intelligence emerge from experience-driven (e.g. [[reinforcement learning]]) and "sub-symbolic" (e.g. [[deep learning]]) procedures that are dynamic and adaptive.

Aligns with symbolic, rule-based approach to machine intelligence:
- [[Michael Bratman]]: 'Intention, Plans, and Practical Reason'
Critics of rule-based approach to machine intelligence:
- [[Hubert Dreyfus]]
- [[Daniel Dennett]]: [[R1]] the symbolic reasoner.

A problem for reason-based actions.

Actors often act for reasons without deliberating. For instance, Ada quickly notices a risk to her cat and reacts appropriately without reflective thought. How can perception, memory, and action be aligned with reasons without conscious deliberation? This calls for a system that can be attuned to reasons automatically.

[[Railton (2017)]] argues that the [[affective system]] continuously and automatically orients attention, primes memory, and assigns value to outcomes. This enables rational responsiveness without conscious deliberation. Thus, actors can be attuned to reasons for action via implicit, emotion-driven mechanisms that still reflect their values and goals.

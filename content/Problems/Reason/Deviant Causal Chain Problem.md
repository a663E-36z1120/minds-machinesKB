A problem for reason-based actions.

An actor’s rationale might cause behavior that matches their intent, but through an unintended path. For example, Ada plans to step near her cat to scare it, but accidentally steps directly on its tail — her idea caused the action, but not in the right way. So, it’s not truly acting for that reason.

[[Peter Railton]] in [[Railton (2017)]] offers a solution:
By grounding action in an internal evaluative model (i.e. the [[affective system]]) that guides behavior prospectively and adjusts based on outcomes, it’s not just about causation — but rather whether the actor’s internal evaluative system represented and tracked the goal in real-time. So, acting “under an idea” is when the model guides the action appropriately, not by accident.

A problem for reason-based actions.

If every reason-based action requires a separate act of “choosing” to act on that reason, we face an infinite regress of actions.

In [[Railton (2017)]], [[Peter Railton]] avoids regress by rejecting the idea that agency requires a separate act of will for each action. Instead, Railton shows that organized, evaluative processes within the [[affective system]] can steer behavior without needing conscious intervention at every step. These processes belong to the actor, even if they are nondeliberative.

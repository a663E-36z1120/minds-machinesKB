An economic game that reflects how moral valuation can be measured, in accordance to [[Moral Valuationism]].

**Gameplay:**
- The **proposer** suggests a split of the money.
- The **responder** can either **accept** or **reject** the offer.
- If the offer is **accepted**, the money is divided as proposed.
- If the offer is **rejected**, both players receive nothing.

**Proposer Behavior:**
- Proposers generally offer between **40% and 50%** of the total sum.
- Offers that are too low (often below **20-30%**) are rare, as they risk rejection.
        
**Responder Behavior:**
- Responders tend to reject offers that they perceive as unfair, frequently those below **20-30%**.
- The willingness to reject low offers indicates that responders are motivated by considerations of fairness and are willing to sacrifice some potential gain to punish the proposer for an inequitable offer.

Population behavior varies across cultures.

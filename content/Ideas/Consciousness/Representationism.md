The theory of [[consciousness]] that attempts to account for a conscious state’s phenomenal character in terms of its representational content.

This position maintains that:
- To be conscious is to be in a state that carries specific information about the external environment or internal conditions. The qualitative feel of an experience (its phenomenology) is explained by the particular content it encodes.
- **Implication:** The nature of conscious experiences is understood in terms of how they represent or “stand for” objects, properties, or states of affairs, linking subjective experience to objective information.

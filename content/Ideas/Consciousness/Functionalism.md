The theory of [[consciousness]] that that attempts to account for the phenomenal character of conscious states in terms of their functional (cause and effect) role (i.e. function).

This position maintains that:
- As long as a system exhibits the right functional organization (i.e., it processes inputs, interacts with other mental states, and produces appropriate outputs), it can be said to possess mental states, regardless of the system’s material substrate.
- Consciousness is multiply realizable; different physical systems (brains, computers, or other substrates) that perform the same function could, in principle, have the same conscious experiences.

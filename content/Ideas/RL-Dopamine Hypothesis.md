A hypothesis that the midbrain VTA/SN neurons in the [[human brain]] directly code for several  components of [[model-free]] [[reinforcement learning]]:
1. selected actions (i.e. outcome of policy)  
2. prediction error (Q-old minus Q-new)
Where dopamine levels modulate the computation required for RL:  
- Higher dopamine should lead to faster learning.
- Lower dopamine to slower learning.

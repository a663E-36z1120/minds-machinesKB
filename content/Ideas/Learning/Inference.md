Inference is a transition from thoughts representing premises to thoughts representing conclusions. Two requirements for inference are:
1. A (positive?) causal relationship between thoughts.
2. An awareness of the nature of the connection.

Inference can be: 
- **Deductive** - premises mean the conclusion must be true.
- **Inductive** - premises mean the conclusion is probable.
- **Abductive** - premises are “best explained” by the conclusion.

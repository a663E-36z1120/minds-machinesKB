[[Julia Haas]] proposes in '*Moral Gridworlds: A Theoretical Proposal for Modeling Artificial Moral Cognition*' (2019), that [[reinforcement learning]] can provide a way to create artificial learners who figure out how to act morally, instead of being pre-programmed to do so. 

Haas argues that to build moral agents, we can:
- Create gridworlds that represent moral problems (such as economic games like the [[Ultimatum Game]])  
- Engineer a reward signal (or signals) that represent moral and non moral value, as well as the optimal trade-off between these.
- Train RL agents on the gridworlds.

**Bayes’ Theorem** (named after [[Thomas Bayes]]) is a mathematical rule for updating beliefs based on new evidence. It connects three key components:

$$
P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)}
$$
Where:
- **Prior** $P(H)$:  
  The initial probability of $H$ before considering the new evidence.  
  (*What you believed before seeing the data.*)
- **Likelihood** $P(E|H)$:  
  The probability of seeing the evidence $E$ assuming the hypothesis $H$ is true.  
  (*How expected the evidence is if the hypothesis holds.*)
- **Posterior** $P(H|E)$:  
  The updated probability of a hypothesis $H$ after observing evidence $E$.  
  (*What you believe after seeing the data.*)

Bayes' Theorem formalizes how rational agents should **revise their beliefs** in light of new information.
